# General / Cross-Sector

ISO/IEC 27001 – International standard for information security management systems (ISMS); widely adopted across all industries
ISO/IEC 27002 – Companion to 27001; provides detailed security controls guidance
NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) – US-originated but globally referenced; sector-agnostic risk management framework
SOC 2 (AICPA) – US standard for service organizations, focused on security, availability, and data handling; heavily used by SaaS providers
CIS Controls – Prioritized set of cybersecurity best practices, widely used as a baseline
GDPR – EU data protection regulation with significant technical implications (encryption, access control, breach notification, data minimization)
eIDAS / eIDAS 2.0 – EU regulation governing electronic identification and trust services
ENISA Guidelines – Non-binding but authoritative technical guidance from the EU cybersecurity agency


# Financial Services (beyond DORA, MiFID, PSD2/3)

Basel III / IV – Banking capital and risk frameworks with indirect IT risk implications (operational risk)
SWIFT CSCF (Customer Security Controls Framework) – Mandatory cybersecurity baseline for all SWIFT network participants
PCI DSS – Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard; technically very prescriptive for any entity handling card data
TIBER-EU / TIBER-DE etc. – ECB-led threat intelligence-based ethical red-teaming framework for financial institutions
EBA Guidelines on ICT and Security Risk Management – Precursor to and complement of DORA
FFIEC (US) – Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council guidelines for US banks covering IT, cybersecurity, and business continuity
SEC Cybersecurity Rules (US) – Disclosure and incident reporting obligations for public companies and investment advisers
GLBA / Safeguards Rule (US) – Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act; requires financial institutions to protect customer data


# Healthcare

HIPAA (US) – Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act; Security Rule sets detailed technical requirements for protected health information (PHI)
HITECH Act (US) – Strengthens HIPAA enforcement; adds breach notification requirements
NIS2 + EHDS – EU eHealth Data Space regulation complements NIS2 for health data infrastructure
MDR / IVDR (EU) – Medical Device Regulation and In Vitro Diagnostic Regulation; include cybersecurity requirements for connected medical devices
IEC 62443 – Industrial and medical device cybersecurity standard (also used in OT/ICS environments broadly)
FDA Cybersecurity Guidance (US) – Pre- and post-market cybersecurity requirements for medical devices
ISO 27799 – Health informatics extension of ISO 27001; sector-specific security guidelines


# Critical Infrastructure & Industrial / OT

IEC 62443 – The primary standard for Operational Technology (OT) and Industrial Control Systems (ICS) security; used in energy, manufacturing, water
NERC CIP (North America) – Mandatory cybersecurity standards for bulk electric system operators
TSA Security Directives (US) – Pipeline and aviation sector cybersecurity mandates post-Colonial Pipeline
NIS2 Annex I & II sectors – Covers energy, transport, water, digital infrastructure, public administration as "essential" or "important" entities


# Telecommunications & Digital Services

EECC / Electronic Communications Code (EU) – Security obligations for telecom providers
BEREC Guidelines – Supplement EECC with technical detail
3GPP Security Standards – Technical security specs for mobile networks (4G/5G)


# Aviation & Transport

ICAO Cybersecurity Strategy – International Civil Aviation Organization framework
EASA AMC/GM on Cybersecurity – European Union Aviation Safety Agency requirements for airworthiness and ground systems
IMO Maritime Cyber Risk Guidelines (MSC-FAL.1/Circ.3) – Applies to shipping and port operators


# Cloud & Technology Providers

CSA CCM (Cloud Controls Matrix) – Cloud Security Alliance framework for cloud service providers
ISO/IEC 27017 & 27018 – Extensions of 27001 specifically for cloud security and cloud privacy
EU Cloud Rulebook / EUCS – Emerging EU certification scheme for cloud services under ENISA
FedRAMP (US) – Mandatory cloud security authorization framework for US federal government suppliers


A few practical observations worth noting:

- Overlap is intentional: DORA explicitly references ISO 27001, NIST, and TIBER-EU. Regulators increasingly build on existing standards rather than reinventing them.
- Extraterritorial reach: GDPR, HIPAA, and SEC rules can apply to non-US/non-EU companies if they serve affected customers or are listed on US exchanges.
- Convergence trend: NIS2 and DORA represent a broader EU move toward harmonized, binding technical requirements rather than voluntary frameworks — expect more sectors to follow this pattern.
